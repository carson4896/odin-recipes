Mango Sticky Rice
Return Home
Description
Mango Sticky Rice is a popular Thai dessert made with glutinous rice, fresh mangoes, and coconut milk. It's a sweet and fragrant treat that's perfect for satisfying your sweet cravings.

Mango Sticky Rice originated in Thailand and has since become a beloved dessert worldwide. It's often enjoyed during the summer months when mangoes are in season.
Measured Ingredients
  • 1 cup glutinous rice (also known as sticky rice)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced
  • Sesame seeds or toasted mung beans, for garnish (optional)
Instructions
  1. Rinse the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the glutinous rice, coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Stir to combine.
  3. Cover the saucepan and let the mixture soak for at least 30 minutes or up to 1 hour.
  4. After soaking, place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  6. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 10 minutes.
  7. While the rice is cooking, prepare the mangoes by peeling and slicing them.
  8. To serve, place a portion of the cooked sticky rice on a plate or bowl. Arrange sliced mangoes on top or alongside the rice.
  9. If desired, garnish with sesame seeds or toasted mung beans.
  10. Enjoy your delicious Mango Sticky Rice!
