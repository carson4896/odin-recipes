Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu (pronounced "tee-ruh-mee-soo") is a classic Italian layered dessert that's easy to make at home with coffee-soaked ladyfingers, a creamy mascarpone custard, and whipped cream. Dust the top of the tiramisu with cocoa powder for an impressive no-bake dessert!

Origin

The famous Italian restauranteur, Ado Campeol, is credited with its invention in the 1970s. The word "tiramisu" translates to "pick-me-up"

Ingredients for a Decadent Tiramisu

  • Egg Yolks: Necessary for a velvety smooth filling.
  • Sugar: Cooked with egg yolks and combined with mascarpone to make a sweet filling.
  • Milk: We recommend whole milk to slightly thin the filling to make it more spreadable.
  • Cream: Beat the heavy cream until stiff peaks form to creaet the whipped cream.
  • Vanilla: Of course you need vanilla for your whipped cream!
  • Mascarpone: This soft Italian cheese is the key ingredient to a classic tiramisu.
  • Coffee: We use a mixture of coffee and rum to soak our ladyfingers to give us the classic tiramisu flavor.
    • Rum (Optional): We can use rum to spike the coffee that we'll use to soak the ladyfingers.
  • Ladyfingers: We like to use these small sponge cakes that are shaped like thick fingers. Pound cake cut into strips is also an effective replacement.
  • Cocoa Powder: Finish off your tiramisue with a dusting of cocoa powder for a beautiful topping aesthetic.
