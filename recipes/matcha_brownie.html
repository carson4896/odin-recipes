Matcha Brownies

Description & Origin
Matcha Brownies combine the rich, fudgy texture of traditional brownies with the earthy flavor of matcha green tea powder. These indulgent treats are perfect for green tea lovers looking for a unique twist on a classic dessert.

While the exact origin of Matcha Brownies is unclear, they are believed to have originated as a modern fusion dessert, combining the popularity of matcha with the timeless appeal of brownies.
Measured Ingredients
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup matcha green tea powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup white chocolate chips (optional)
Instructions
  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour an 8x8-inch baking pan.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the butter. Allow it to cool slightly.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together melted butter and sugar until well combined.
  4. Beat in eggs, one at a time, until fully incorporated. Stir in vanilla extract.
  5. In a separate bowl, sift together flour, matcha powder, and salt.
  6. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until just combined.
  7. If desired, fold in white chocolate chips.
  8. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and spread it evenly.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with moist crumbs.
  10. Allow the brownies to cool in the pan before cutting into squares.
  11. Serve and enjoy these delicious Matcha Brownies!
