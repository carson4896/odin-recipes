Description & Origin
Banana Pudding is a beloved Southern dessert made with layers of creamy pudding, sliced bananas, and vanilla wafers. It's a comforting and nostalgic treat that's perfect for any occasion. The exact origin of Banana Pudding is unclear, but it gained popularity in the United States, particularly in the South, in the early 20th century.

Measured Ingredients
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 box vanilla wafers
  • 4 ripe bananas, sliced
Instructions
  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together sugar, flour, and salt. Gradually whisk in milk until smooth.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until mixture thickens and boils. Boil and stir for 1 minute.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk egg yolks. Gradually whisk in 1 cup of hot mixture.
  4. Return all to saucepan, whisking constantly. Bring to a gentle boil and cook for 1 more minute.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla extract.
  6. Layer one-third of vanilla wafers in a 2-quart serving dish. Top with half of sliced bananas and half of pudding mixture.
  7. Repeat layers, ending with pudding mixture.
  8. Chill for at least 4 hours before serving.
