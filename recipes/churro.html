Churros
Return Home
Description
Churros are a classic Spanish dessert consisting of fried dough pastry dusted with cinnamon sugar. They are crispy on the outside and soft and fluffy on the inside, making them utterly irresistible.

Churros originated in Spain and Portugal and have since become popular in many countries around the world, especially in Latin America. They are commonly enjoyed as a snack or dessert.
Measured Ingredients
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for coating)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (for coating)
Instructions
  1. In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, salt, and vegetable oil. Bring to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Remove the saucepan from the heat and add the flour all at once. Stir vigorously until the mixture forms a ball and pulls away from the sides of the pan.
  3. Transfer the dough to a piping bag fitted with a large star tip.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Pipe 4-6 inch strips of dough into the hot oil, using scissors or a knife to cut the dough from the piping bag.
  6. Fry the churros until golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  7. In a shallow dish, combine sugar and cinnamon for coating.
  8. While the churros are still warm, roll them in the cinnamon sugar mixture until evenly coated.
  9. Serve the churros warm with your favorite dipping sauce, such as chocolate sauce or dulce de leche.
  10. Enjoy these delicious homemade Churros!
